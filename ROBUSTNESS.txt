PART A
1. Fit a GARCH model to the series so volatility clustering is modeled explicitly.

2. pply EVT / GPD on the standardized residuals from that GARCH fit—not on raw 
returns—so tail risk is estimated after removing the predictable volatility dynamics.


So the picture is arguing for conditional EVT: GARCH for the volatility path, 
EVT on residuals for tail / extreme-loss modeling (typical in VaR / ES–style work).



For Part B: 
Q. Develop and implement a method to estimate the same 1-month, 3-month, and 6-month 
shocks under this data limitation. (TAIL ESTIMATION)
The following steps:
- Construct month-end horizon returns and convert to losses
- Choose Threshold
- Fit a Generalized Pareto Distribution to losses above threshold (Extreme Value Theorem).
- Use the fitted tail to estimate the desired adverse quantiles.
